Sports Index measures **attention and influence** for every FBS college football athlete. We index news and social coverage from a published set of sources, attribute every story to the athletes and programs it covers, and turn that corpus into rankings, statistics, AI-written daily stories, and per-athlete intelligence pages — alternative data for the NIL era.

## Who it's for

- **Collectives & brands** — understand which athletes command attention before committing NIL dollars
- **Media & analysts** — a ranked, filterable view of the sport's coverage landscape
- **Programs & athletes** — see and claim your page; track your own coverage and reach
- **Fans** — follow the athletes, programs, and stories driving college football

## Key concepts

### Athlete & team pages

Every tracked athlete and program has a public page at `sportsindex.io/topic/{name}` aggregating their coverage, statistics, social reach, and media — generated and updated automatically. Athletes can [claim their page](/guide/topics/claiming) for editorial control.

### The Influence Leaderboard

The ranked view of college football attention: follower bases, growth, and coverage volume for 6,000+ Power 4 athletes, at [sportsindex.io/leaderboard](https://sportsindex.io/leaderboard).

### Indexed coverage & attribution

Every story and post we ingest flows through an attribution engine that links it to the athletes and programs it covers. Browse the full corpus — filterable by player, school, conference, source, and date — in the [Newsroom](https://sportsindex.io/newsroom). See [How the Index Works](/guide/methodology) for the methodology.
